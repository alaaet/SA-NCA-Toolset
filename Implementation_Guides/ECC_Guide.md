## üèóÔ∏è The 4 Pillars of ECC
The ECC is built on four massive pillars. Memorize them. Tattoo them on your arm (figuratively).

### 1. Governance (The "Strategy" Stuff)
*   **Strategy**: You need a plan. "We'll figure it out later" is not a strategy.
*   **Roles & Responsibilities**: Who gets fired when things go wrong? (Ideally, no one, because you assigned roles correctly).
*   **Policy**: Write it down. If it's not written down, it doesn't exist.
*   **HR**: Background checks. NDAs. Training. Don't give full admin access to the intern on day 1.

### 2. Defense (The "Technical" Stuff)
*   **Asset Management**: You can't protect what you can't see.
*   **Identity (IAM)**: Identification, Authentication, Authorization. The holy trinity.
*   **Encryption**: Encrypt data at rest, in motion, and in use (if you're fancy).
*   **Vulnerability Management**: Patch your stuff. Please.
*   **Network Security**: Firewalls, segmentation, and keeping the bad guys out.

### 3. Resilience (The "Oh No" Stuff)
*   **Incident Response**: When (not if) you get hacked, what do you do? Panic is not a valid step.
*   **Business Continuity**: Keep the lights on.

### 4. Third-Party (The "Vendor" Stuff)
*   **Supply Chain**: Your vendor's security is your security. If they fail, you fail.
*   **Cloud**: (See our separate CCC Guide for this beast).

## üéØ The "Must-Dos" (Top Priorities)
You have **108 Main Controls**. That's a lot. Here is where you start:

1.  **Form a Committee**: You need a Steering Committee. Get the big bosses in a room.
2.  **Asset Inventory**: Build a spreadsheet (or use a tool) of every server, laptop, and application.
3.  **Patch Management**: Automate this. You cannot patch 500 servers manually before the next exploit hits.
4.  **MFA**: Multi-Factor Authentication everywhere. No excuses.
